Where HOMES & HEAR fit in
Context

The Home Energy Rebate programs bring new federal funding into Oregon for **whole-home efficiency** and **electrification**. The Oregon Department of Energy is the grantee; Energy Trust of Oregon and Earth Advantage will run most customer-facing delivery.

CBOs aren‚Äôt expected to become energy auditors or contractors. The main ask is to **help people get in the door**, understand the steps, and not miss out because of paperwork, language, or trust barriers.

  • HOMES = deeper, modeled savings with trade ally assessors and modelers.
  • HEAR = measure-based rebates for LMI households (e.g., heat pumps, HPWH, stoves, panel).
  • Both rely on **closed trade ally networks** and **income verification**.

Treat this hub as a living explainer. Final rules and RFQ language will come through official channels.

RFQ to CBOs
What the partnership is about
RFQ framing

The RFQ will look for CBOs that can act as **trusted guides** for households that may struggle with technical processes and documentation. The work is not one-size-fits-all; the RFQ is likely to allow CBOs to choose which functions they‚Äôre best suited for.

  • Outreach & education in specific communities.
  • Intake & eligibility screening, including income and housing type.
  • Navigation through trade ally networks and program steps.
  • Documentation support and light case management.
  • Support for stacking HOMES/HEAR with other funding.
  • Feedback to Energy Trust on barriers and improvements.

Think about what you already do well (e.g., tenant support, energy education, case management) and which elements map cleanly to these asks.

FAQs for CBOs
Quick answers to common questions
Do we have to understand every technical rule? ‚ñ∂
No. Implementers and contractors will handle the deep technical modeling and eligibility logic. CBOs should have a **working understanding** of who the programs are for, the basic steps, and where your community members tend to get stuck.
Will CBOs receive funding for this work? ‚ñ∂
Yes, the RFQ is intended to establish **funded partnerships**. Exact amounts, payment structures, and reporting expectations will be set out in the formal RFQ and contracts, not in this explainer.
How risky is the data/PII side for us? ‚ñ∂
You will likely be supporting income verification and other sensitive documents. That means you will need **clear privacy policies**, secure systems, and staff training. If your current systems are not ready, you can still participate by focusing on outreach and navigation while you improve your data setup.
Can we partner as a coalition? ‚ñ∂
Coalitions are strongly encouraged. For example, one CBO might lead on administration and contracting, while others focus on specific communities or geographies.
What if we already run energy or housing programs? ‚ñ∂
That‚Äôs a plus. The goal is to **integrate HOMES/HEAR into existing support ecosystems**, not to bolt on a completely separate, confusing process. The RFQ will want to see how you connect these dots.
What should we do now, before the RFQ is out? ‚ñ∂
Start mapping:
  • Which communities you would focus on.
  • What parts of the scope you can realistically own.
  • What data/privacy upgrades you might need.
  • Which partners (CBOs, utilities, contractors) you‚Äôd want to team up with.
That prep will make your RFQ response and onboarding much smoother.
